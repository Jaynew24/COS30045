TV Energy Usage Guide

Televisions have evolved significantly in recent years, with energy efficiency varying greatly between different technologies and models.

LED TVs

Most energy-efficient TV technology available today. Uses up to 50% less power than LCD TVs of similar size.

Average consumption: 50-100 watts

OLED TVs

Superior picture quality but higher energy consumption than LED, especially with brighter content.

Average consumption: 100-200 watts

QLED TVs

Quantum dot technology offers vibrant colors with moderate energy consumption between LED and OLED.

Average consumption: 70-150 watts

Energy Saving Tips for TVs

  • Enable automatic brightness adjustment based on room lighting
  • Turn off rather than leaving on standby when not in use
  • Choose a size appropriate for your viewing distance - larger screens consume more power
  • Look for high energy star ratings when purchasing new TVs
