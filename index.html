Popular Appliances

Televisions

Modern TVs vary greatly in energy consumption. LED TVs are more efficient than Plasma or OLED models.

Average consumption: 80-400 watts

Kitchen Appliances

Refrigerators, ovens, and dishwashers are among the highest energy consumers in most households.

Average consumption: 500-2000 watts

Cooling & Heating

Air conditioners and heaters account for approximately 40% of household energy use in Australia.

Average consumption: 1000-5000 watts

Understanding Energy Consumption in Australia

Australian households spend an average of $1,500 per year on electricity, with appliances accounting for about 30% of this cost. With rising energy prices, understanding how to optimize your appliance usage can lead to significant savings.

Our website provides practical information to help you make informed decisions about your appliance usage and purchases.

Television Energy Consumption

TV Energy Usage Guide

Televisions have evolved significantly in recent years, with energy efficiency varying greatly between different technologies and models.

LED TVs

Most energy-efficient TV technology available today. Uses up to 50% less power than LCD TVs of similar size.

Average consumption: 50-100 watts

OLED TVs

Superior picture quality but higher energy consumption than LED, especially with brighter content.

Average consumption: 100-200 watts

QLED TVs

Quantum dot technology offers vibrant colors with moderate energy consumption between LED and OLED.

Average consumption: 70-150 watts

Energy Saving Tips for TVs

  • Enable automatic brightness adjustment based on room lighting
  • Turn off rather than leaving on standby when not in use
  • Choose a size appropriate for your viewing distance - larger screens consume more power
  • Look for high energy star ratings when purchasing new TVs
